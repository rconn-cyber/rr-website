The Story Begins Here

Tampa, Florida — Spring 1898

In the spring of 1898, Tampa became the staging ground for one of the most celebrated military campaigns in American history. The city's port, its cigar-scented streets, and the grand Plant Hotel bore witness to the gathering of the legendary Rough Riders.

May 1898 — Tampa, Florida

The Rough Riders Arrive

Congress authorized the raising of three volunteer cavalry regiments on April 22, 1898. Theodore Roosevelt, then Assistant Secretary of the Navy, resigned his post and accepted a commission as Lieutenant Colonel. Over 23,000 men applied for just 1,000 slots. The regiment was a unique mix of Ivy League athletes, cowboys, miners, and Native Americans — united by Roosevelt's call to adventure.

May–June 1898 — The Plant Hotel

Headquarters at the Tampa Bay Hotel

Henry Plant's magnificent Tampa Bay Hotel — a Moorish Revival palace of 511 rooms — served as headquarters for the Army officer corps. Colonel Leonard Wood and Lieutenant Colonel Roosevelt set up operations here. The hotel's verandas became the social center of the war effort, where reporters, officers, and society figures mingled in an atmosphere of patriotic excitement. Today the building stands as the University of Tampa.

May–June 1898 — Ybor City & Tampa

Training in the Streets of Tampa

The Rough Riders drilled in the streets and open fields around Tampa. The cobblestone lanes of Ybor City — the cigar capital of the world — echoed with cavalry hooves. Tampa's Cuban exile community, veterans of their own struggle for independence, watched with deep interest and shared intelligence about Cuba's terrain. The soldiers frequented the cafés and cantinas of Ybor, forging bonds between the regiment and the city.

June 1898 — Port Tampa

Departure from Old Port Tampa

After weeks of logistical chaos and sweltering heat, the regiment received embarkation orders. In a bold maneuver, Roosevelt commandeered the transport ship S.S. Yucatan, securing berths for his men over competing regiments. On June 14, 1898, the Rough Riders departed from Port Tampa, heading south for Cuba and their rendezvous with destiny at San Juan Hill.

July 1, 1898 — Cuba

The Charge Up San Juan Hill

The charge up Kettle Hill and the San Juan Heights on July 1, 1898 made Roosevelt and the Rough Riders legendary. Though they had to leave their horses in Tampa (the cavalry fought on foot), their courage was undimmed. Roosevelt personally led the charge on horseback through enemy fire — the last great cavalry charge of the American military. He would later call it "the great day of my life."

Period Photographs

Tampa, 1898 — Through the Lens

Rare photographs documenting the gathering of the Rough Riders in Tampa — the hotels, the streets, the men, and the moment.

The Tampa Bay Hotel (Plant Hotel), 1898

Henry Plant's grand Moorish Revival palace served as Army headquarters during the mobilization. Col. Roosevelt and senior officers directed operations from its ornate verandas. Now the University of Tampa.

Rough Riders Staff, Tampa, 1898

Officers and staff of the 1st U.S. Volunteer Cavalry Regiment photographed in Tampa. The regiment drew from every corner of America — cowboys, Ivy League athletes, Native Americans, and city police.

The Charge — San Juan Hill, July 1, 1898

The Rough Riders' charge up Kettle Hill and the San Juan Heights made Roosevelt and his regiment immortal. He later called it "the great day of my life." The victory broke the Spanish defenses around Santiago.

Lt. Colonel Theodore Roosevelt, 1898

"TR" in his custom Rough Riders uniform, complete with his trademark spectacles. His conspicuousness under fire was legendary — and to the bewilderment of his men, he appeared to enjoy it thoroughly.

Roosevelt at Tampa, Florida, 1898

Roosevelt photographed as a Rough Rider in Tampa before the regiment's departure. He resigned as Assistant Secretary of the Navy to lead these men — one of the most consequential decisions of his life.

Colonel Roosevelt with His Regiment

Roosevelt with officers of the 1st U.S. Volunteer Cavalry — photographed by Frances Benjamin Johnston, one of America's first female photojournalists who covered the regiment extensively in 1898.

Located at 601 N. 19th St., Tampa, FL

Teddy Roosevelt Museum & Library

The Teddy Roosevelt Museum and Library, housed at the historic Tampa Rough Riders Clubhouse, is one of the nation's most significant collections dedicated to the Spanish-American War and the 1st U.S. Volunteer Cavalry Regiment.

Our collection spans original weapons, uniforms, insignia, campaign medals, personal correspondence, period photographs, regimental flags, and an extensive reference library — all carefully preserved by the 630+ member organization that has guarded this legacy for decades.

Collection Highlights

  • Original 1898 Krag-Jørgensen carbines & Colt revolvers
  • Period Rough Riders uniforms, campaign hats & insignia
  • Campaign medals and military decorations
  • Roosevelt-era personal correspondence & documents
  • Regimental flags and unit battle standards
  • Extensive collection of 1898 Tampa period photographs
  • Spanish-American War reference library (400+ volumes)
  • Tampa Bay Hotel memorabilia from 1898
  • Original Teddy Bear artifacts and Roosevelt memorabilia
